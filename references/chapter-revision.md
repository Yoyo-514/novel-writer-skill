# Scene and Chapter Revision

Use this reference when the user asks for critique, rewrite, polish, edit, revise, improve, or "改稿" on an existing scene or chapter.

Revision order follows the same small-to-large logic:

```text
fix scene -> reassemble chapter -> check volume effect
```

Do not rewrite the outline to match a drifting draft unless the user explicitly approves a plan change.

## Inputs

- the scene/chapter text
- author intent notes
- relevant scene/chapter/volume plan
- character bible
- character index/per-character files for medium/long projects
- world, ability, item, weapon, and terminology files relevant to the text
- style settings and topic profile
- universal prose constraints from `novel-style.md`
- known foreshadowing or future payoffs

If the text is partial, say so briefly and still work from the available material.

## Missing Text / Plan Gate

If the user asks for a rewrite or polish but provides no usable scene/chapter text, do not invent a full rewrite. Offer 2-3 rewrite direction options or ask for the source text.

If the text exists but the needed scene/chapter plan is missing, continue with critique anchored to the text, but propose plan options before making structural rewrites that would create new canon.

## Revision Passes

### 1. Reading Friction

Read as the target reader and list concrete resistance points:

- false, awkward, flat, rushed, confusing, or underfed lines
- actions that feel unlike the character
- dialogue that feels too functional
- pacing that over-compresses or drags
- reward beats that do not land
- depth opportunities that vanish too quickly

Anchor each point to a quoted phrase or clear location. Keep this grounded in the text.

### 2. Fix Map

For each friction point, identify the likely craft cause and an operation:

- causality: add setup, reorder beat, clarify result
- motivation: add pressure, fear, want, or hidden reason
- conflict: make stakes personal, add obstacle, add consequence
- dialogue: add subtext, information imbalance, evasion, voice difference
- prose constraint: remove repeated negation, denial-to-affirmation patterns, arbitrary clipped dialogue, analytical narration, needless turns, awkward intensifiers, disruptive line breaks, and overused em dashes
- worldbuilding: move explanation into action, cost, rule, object, or institution
- ability system: restore trigger, cost, limit, mastery, counterplay, or reveal boundary
- terminology: clarify in-world meaning through context, reduce term overload, or separate it from translation glossary concerns
- pacing: expand anchor moment, cut transition, split scene, move reveal
- depth: add emotional, cognitive, or thematic layer to a key event
- profile fit: restore light-novel reward, CP chemistry, cinematic image, action readability, or worldbuilding payoff

Also mark:

- foreshadowing to protect
- effective jokes/Neta to keep
- character charm worth amplifying
- scenes that need only minimal edits

### 3. Revised Text

Choose the least invasive edit that solves the real problem:

- light issue: local polish
- medium issue: rewrite a paragraph or exchange
- scene issue: restructure the scene
- chapter issue: revise scene order or ending interface

Priorities:

1. fix logic, motivation, and depth problems
2. protect foreshadowing and useful ambiguity
3. preserve working ACG/light-novel texture
4. keep prose readable
5. strengthen key moments with layered meaning
6. enforce universal prose constraints
7. polish wording after structure works

### 4. Change Notes

End with 3-5 notes:

- what changed
- why it helps
- what the author may still tune
- whether the chapter or volume plan is affected

## Chinese Light Novel Revision Checks

- Does the scene/chapter deliver a clear reader reward?
- Is the cute/sweet/funny/satisfying moment triggered by situation?
- Does inner commentary reveal personality or humor instead of repeating surface emotion?
- Does daily life do work?
- Does relationship chemistry have friction and progression?
- Are jokes and Neta natural in context?
- Do special terms, abilities, or setting details support character interaction instead of drowning it?

## Cinematic Hybrid Revision Checks

- Does the scene have a clear visual anchor?
- Does a flashback or montage change meaning rather than decorate?
- Is action spatially readable and emotionally meaningful?
- Does worldbuilding affect choices or consequences?
- Are ability, item, weapon, and location details visually clear and causally meaningful?
- Does a slow section provide enough interim reward?

## World / Ability / Terminology Revision Checks

- Does the scene contradict core world rules, location limits, faction behavior, or item/weapon state?
- Does any ability solve a problem too easily or ignore established cost, limit, trigger, mastery, or counterplay?
- Does the text introduce a recurring term without adding or updating the term index?
- Does the text confuse in-world terminology with translation glossary choices?
- Does a character use world knowledge, terms, items, or abilities they should not know or possess?
- Has any setting change affected later outline logic, foreshadowing, or prior chapters?

## Universal Prose Constraint Checks

- Does the text rely on `没有...只是...`, `不是...而是...`, repeated `不...不...`, or similar rhetorical contrast habits?
- Are motives explained when behavior could show them?
- Does the narration summarize, evaluate, or conclude instead of dramatizing?
- Are dialogue lines clipped into unnatural fragments without character or dramatic reason?
- Are degree adverbs used awkwardly with nouns?
- Are em dashes, line breaks, or punctuation creating artificial rhythm?
- Does any metaphor or comparison feel decorative rather than perceptual?

## Output Shape

For full revision:

1. `阅读阻力`
2. `修订方案`
3. `修订稿`
4. `改动说明`

For quick polish, compress the first two sections but keep the logic visible unless the user asks for rewrite-only output.
