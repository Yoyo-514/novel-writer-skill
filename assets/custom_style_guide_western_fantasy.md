# Custom Style Guide - Western Epic Fantasy in Chinese

## Creative Direction

Write Chinese prose with the weight and distance of Western epic fantasy: old kingdoms, roads, ruins, vows, borders, and histories that feel larger than the current cast.

## Voice

- Elevated but readable Chinese.
- Avoid modern slang and internet phrasing.
- Use concrete sensory details before abstract emotion.
- Let dialogue carry conflict, negotiation, confession, and withheld truth.

## Worldbuilding

- Keep pre-industrial vocabulary unless the setting explicitly includes modern technology.
- Replace modern technical phrasing with period-appropriate description.
- Treat non-human peoples as cultures, not decorations.

## Forbidden in Prose

- author notes
- meta commentary
- "the reader" or "the audience"
- writing-process terms such as foreshadowing, character arc, or narrative tension
- summary endings for scenes that deserve dramatization
